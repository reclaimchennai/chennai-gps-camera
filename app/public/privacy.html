Privacy Policy

Chennai GPS Camera (city.reclaimchennai.cam) · Last updated 19 July 2026

Chennai GPS Camera is a privacy-first GPS/location-stamp camera. It is built so that your photos, videos, and location stay on your device. We do not run accounts, we do not have a server that receives your media, and we do not use analytics, advertising, or tracking of any kind.

The short version: Everything the app produces — photos, videos, and the location watermark — is created and stored locally on your phone. Nothing is uploaded to us. The only times any data leaves your device are the clearly-listed, on-demand cases below.

What the app accesses, and why

  • Camera — to show the viewfinder and take photos and videos. Images are processed on-device and saved to your phone.
  • Microphone — to record audio for videos and to show the live sound-level (dB) reading on the watermark. Audio is processed on-device; the meter reading is a number, not a recording.
  • Location (GPS) — to stamp coordinates, address, DIGIPIN, and jurisdiction (ward / zone / police limits) onto your photos and videos. Jurisdiction is resolved using map data that ships inside the app, fully offline.
  • Photos / storage — to save your captures to your device gallery (on Android 9 and below this uses a storage permission; newer versions save via the system media store).

When data leaves your device

By default the app works offline. Data is sent off your device only in these specific, functional cases:

  • Address lookup (reverse geocoding). To turn your coordinates into a street address for the watermark, the app may send those coordinates to a mapping service: OpenStreetMap Nominatim by default, or Google / MapmyIndia (Mappls) if you supply your own API key in Settings. Only the coordinates are sent — no photo, no identity. This happens only when you are online and address lookup is enabled. It is not used to identify you and the request is not stored by us.
  • Optional online mini-map. If you enable the Google static-map upgrade and provide a Google API key, coordinates are sent to Google to fetch a map thumbnail.
  • Sending us a bug report. If you use “Report an issue,” the message you type — and the annotated screenshot you choose to attach — is sent to the developers over Telegram so we can help. This is entirely initiated by you, and only includes what you put in the report.

The app also downloads its own offline map/data packs from its website (cam.reclaimchennai.city). These are anonymous static-file requests and contain none of your data.

What we collect

Nothing. We operate no backend that receives your photos, videos, location history, or usage. There are no user accounts, no advertising IDs, and no third-party analytics SDKs in the app.

Data retention & deletion

Your captures live in your device’s gallery and in the app’s local storage. You control them: delete them from the in-app gallery or your phone’s gallery, or clear the app’s storage / uninstall the app to remove everything. Because we hold none of your data, there is nothing for us to delete on a server.

Children

The app is a general-purpose utility and is not directed at children. It collects no personal data and has no accounts.

Third-party services

When you use the optional online features above, your request is subject to that provider’s privacy policy: OpenStreetMap Foundation, Google, or MapmyIndia (Mappls) for geocoding/maps, and Telegram for bug reports you choose to send.

Changes
